## Course Description

What counts as a social problem? Are classism, racism, sexism, or heteronormativity types of social problems? What about enduring crises such as homelessness, opioid abuse, drug addiction, or inequalities in education, criminal justice, or healthcare?

In this course, students learn to identify and explain social problems in society. Drawing on empirical evidence and current events, the course examines the real manifestations of social problems and develops an understanding of solutions through policy and social action.


## Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Understand how systems of power create social problems
2. Critically examine social problems, and how they are interconnected and socially constructed
3. Synthesize how social problems affect different communities
4. View themselves as social actors and agents of change
5. Identify organizations devoted to solving pressing social problems

## Assignments

### Course Preparation Module (30 pts)
Completed during Week 1. Includes verifying the email policy, updating your Canvas profile, and posting a video introduction on the class discussion board.

### Course Engagement (50 pts)
Consistent, active engagement with course content throughout the semester. Engagement is assessed through regular Canvas logins and access to weekly course materials.

### Quizzes (30 pts — 3 quizzes)
Three quizzes distributed across the semester to assess mastery of course content.

### Video Journal Entries (40 pts — 4 entries)
Four brief video journal entries recorded throughout the semester. Each entry addresses a topic covered in the course or reflects on a current piece of media related to social problems. Students document their developing engagement with course material.

### Final Presentation (50 pts)
A culminating presentation in which students identify an advocacy organization or nonprofit in San Diego and present a plan for collaborating with that organization to help remedy one social problem covered in the course.

Extra credit is assessed at the end of the course. See Canvas for details.
